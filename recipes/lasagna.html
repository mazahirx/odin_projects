Lasagna Recipe

HomePage

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich layers of pasta, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. Its origins trace back to ancient Rome, but the version most people recognize today developed in Naples during the Middle Ages. Traditional lasagna features alternating sheets of pasta with a hearty ragù made from ground beef or pork, tomato sauce, and spices, all topped with a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. The dish is baked until golden and bubbling, creating a comforting, flavorful meal enjoyed worldwide.

Modern variations of lasagna have expanded far beyond the classic meat-based recipe. Vegetarian versions often include ingredients like spinach, mushrooms, zucchini, and ricotta, while some recipes swap pasta sheets for vegetables such as eggplant or zucchini slices to create a low-carb alternative. Lasagna is also popular in frozen form, making it a convenient option for busy households. Whether served at a family dinner, holiday gathering, or casual get-together, lasagna remains a beloved staple that brings people together with its warm, hearty appeal.

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Onion
  • Cloves
  • Tomato
  • Pasta
  • Basil
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Olive Oil

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onion and cook until soft, about 3–4 minutes.
  3. Stir in garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add ground beef (or beef and pork mixture). Cook until browned. Drain excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  6. Simmer on low heat for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
